Abstract

Let \((X, *, 0)\)(X,∗,0) be a d-algebra and \(\alpha , \beta \)α,β are endomorphisms on X. Motivated by some results on derivations, \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation in rings, and the generalizations of BCK and BCI-algebras, in this paper, we introduce the notion of \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivations on d-algebras, construct several examples and investigate some simple and important results.

Introduction

The study of derivations in rings though initiated long back, but got impetus only after Posner [11] who in 1957, established two very striking results on derivations in prime rings. BCI-algebra has been developed from BCI-logic on the similar way as Boolean algebra was developed from Boolean logic. Imai and Iseki in [3, 4], introduced two classes of abstract algebras BCK-algebras and BCI-algebras and have been extensively investigated by many researchers. They have shown that the class of BCI-algebra is a proper subclass of BCK-algebra.

In [9], the authors introduced the notion of d-algebras which is another useful generalization of BCK-algebras. They investigated several relations between d-algebras and BCK-algebras as well as some other interesting relations between d-algebras and oriented groups. In [9, 10] Jun et al., discussed ideal theory in d-algebras and introduced the notions of d-algebras, d-ideal and investigated some relations among them. Jun and Xin in [5], applied the notion of derivation in ring and near ring theory to BCI-algebras and they also introduced a new concept called a regular derivation in BCI-algebras. They investigated some of its properties, defined a d-invariant ideal and gave conditions for an ideal to be d-invariant. In non-commutative rings, the notion of derivations is extended to d-derivations, left derivations and central derivations.

Recently, several authors [1, 8] have studied \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivations in BCI-algebras and f-derivation on BCC-algebras [7]. Very recently, Kandaraj and Chandramouleeswaran [6] introduced the notion of left f-derivations of d-algebras and obtained some properties.

Motivated by the notion of \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation in the theory of rings, in this paper, we introduce the notion of \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivations on d-algebras and investigate related properties. We provide a condition for a \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation to be regular. We also introduce the concepts of a \(d_{(\alpha ,\beta )}\)d(α,β)-invariant \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation, \(\alpha \)α-ideal and \(\beta \)β-ideal and then we investigate their relations. Furthermore, we obtain some results on regular \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivations. Finally, we construct several examples and establish some more interesting results.

Definition 2.1

[9] A d-algebra is a non -empty set X with a constant 0 and a binary operation \(*\)∗ satisfying the following axioms:

(1) \(x *x = 0\)x∗x=0
(2) \(0 *x = 0\)0∗x=0
(3) \(x *y = 0\)x∗y=0 and \(y *x = 0 \Rightarrow x = y\)y∗x=0⇒x=y.

\((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation on d-algebra

In this section we introduce the notion of \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation of d-algebra and give some examples and results.

Notation For any x, y in a d-algebra \((X, *, 0)\)(X,∗,0) we write \(x*y\)x∗y simply by xy. For elements x and y of a d-algebra X denote \(x \wedge y = y(yx)\)x∧y=y(yx).

Definition 3.1

A mapping \(\alpha \)α of a d-algebra X into itself is called an endomorphism if \(\alpha (x*y) = \alpha (x) * \alpha (y)\)α(x∗y)=α(x)∗α(y). Note that \(\alpha (0) =0\)α(0)=0.

Remark 3.1

Let X be an edge d-algebra and \(\alpha \)α be an endomorphism on X. Then \(\alpha (x)0 = \alpha (x)\)α(x)0=α(x) for any \(x \in X\)x∈X.

Definition 3.2

Let X be a d-algebra. A map \(g : X \longrightarrow X\)g:X⟶X is a left-right derivation (briefly, (l, r) -derivation ) of X, if it satisfies the identity \(g(xy) = g(x)y \wedge xg(y)\)g(xy)=g(x)y∧xg(y) for all \(x, y \in X\)x,y∈X. If g satisfies the identity \(g(xy) = xg(y) \wedge g(x)y\)g(xy)=xg(y)∧g(x)y for all \(x, y \in X\)x,y∈X, then g is a right–left derivation (briefly, (r, l) -derivation) of X. Moreover, if g is both a (l, r)- and (r, l)-derivation, then g is a derivation of X.

Definition 3.3

Let X be a d-algebra. A map \(g : X \longrightarrow X\)g:X⟶X is called a left–right \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation (briefly, (l, r) -\((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation ) of X, if it satisfies the identity \(g(xy) = g(x)\alpha (y) \wedge \beta (x)g(y)\)g(xy)=g(x)α(y)∧β(x)g(y) for all \(x, y \in X\)x,y∈X. If g satisfies the identity \(g(xy) = \alpha (x)g(y) \wedge g(x)\beta (y)\)g(xy)=α(x)g(y)∧g(x)β(y) for all \(x, y \in X\)x,y∈X , then g is called a right–left \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation (briefly, (r, l) - \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation) of X. Moreover, if g is both a (l, r)- and (r, l)-\((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation, then g is an \((\alpha ,\beta )\)(α,β)-derivation of X.
